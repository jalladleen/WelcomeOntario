Driving


Getting or a driver's license in Ontario may be considered a long process. However the government of Ontario has made the steps clear and provided a variety of resources to help throughout the process through refining the system. I hope this page will be a useful resource for you to learn the necessary information to gain your license.



The three different licenses in Ontario



There are 3 exams in the Graduated Licensing program that you need to pass in order to get you an Ontario Driver's License. Each passed exam will lessen the restrictions on your ability to drive in the province.


G1


It is obtained through passing a written exam on the rules of the road, approx. $160 You are only allowed to take it when you turn 16 years old.

G2


A practical exam that is obtained through passing a driving test on the main roads and neighbourhoods, approx. 90$ You can take it after 8 to 12 months of getting your G1

G


It is another practical and final exam that is obtained through passing a driving test on the highway, approx. $90 You can do it after another 12 months from whenn you have obtained your G2

An advice: Try completing a driving lessons program since it will help you to lower your insurance rates. It shows the insurance company that you have recieved a professional training which assures them that you are able to drive safe.

If you plan to use a foreign driver’s licence in Canada, you should get an International Driving Permit (IDP) in your home country. An IDP will give you a translation of your licence into French and English.



Restrictions


There are specific restrictions under Ontario Law that the drivers have to follow; these restrictions depend on your age and current level of the driver's license. The restrictions include information about the: legally permitted blood alcohol level, the orientation and seatbelt usage of passengers, eligible time of the day a person can drive, and what types of road a driver is allowed to drive on.


G1 Restrictions

All ages

  • You are NOT allowed to be operating a vehicle with any alcohol in your blood
  • All passenger MUST be wearing a functional seat belt
  • You'r NOT allowed to drive on any 400-series highways or expressways unless you have a certified driver's instructor with you while you are driving
  • You CAN'T drive unless there is a passenger in the other front seat who has atleast 4 years experience of driving and has a blood alcohol level below 0.5 -- The passenger's blood alcohol level must be zero if they are 21 years old or younger.
G2 Restrictions

19 Unders

  • For the first six months: between 12:00 am and 5:00 am, you are >ONLY allowed to have one passenger who is 19 years old or under in case you do not have a fully licensed driver with you in the car.
  • After that, you are allowed up to three.
  • These restrictions do not apply to immediate family members.
