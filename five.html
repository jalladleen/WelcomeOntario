Universities


Ontario is known for having many of the highest ranked universities around the world. Many of Ontario's universities have a variety of programs that are delivered with high quality and student-oriented systems.



Programs offered



Undergraduate


Undergraduate degrees usually need 3 years to complete. A 4th or “honours” year is usually needed if you want to be admitted into a graduate program.

Graduate degrees


Usually, a previous undergraduate (bachelor's) degree, or the equivalent is needed to apply for a graduate program. A graduate program is a higher level of university study which may result in a masters or doctorate degree.

Co-op programs


They include periods of employment in related industries within the academic year. They are scheduled by the university and the given placements are paid positions and may be one of the requirements for graduation.

Professional programs


A program of advanced learning that leads to an occupation managed by a required regulatory body. The profession’s members must complete a licensing exam prior being able to actively practice. Besdies, they have to keep their credentials current, through more education. Professional programs include medicine, law, teacher education, dentistry, etc.

All universities offer undergraduate (bachelor) degrees and most offer graduate (master’s and doctoral) degrees.


Applications



There are different ways to apply:




Ontario high school students

You can apply through the Ontario Universities’ Application Centre. In October or November, your high school will give you an access code letter that has information on how to apply.

Timeline for the application process:

  1. Late August

    2. The OUAC launches OUInfo (A helpful website to learn about programs, universities, finances, applications, etc.) and starts to distribute the materials for the 101 application to Ontario high schools and school boards.

  2. By the end of Novembert

    3. Students will receive their application access codes with their school number, student number and temporary PIN. Note: you need this information to be significantly essential to apply to universities.

  3. Mid January (Normally Jan 15)

    4. It is usually the deadline for Ontario high school students to submit their completed application/(s) to the OUAC.

  4. Mid January (Normally Jan 15)

    5. It is usually the deadline for Ontario high school students to submit their completed application/(s) to the OUAC.

Canadian students not living in Ontario

You will need to fill out a 105D online application through the Ontario Universities’ Application Centre.

Timeline for the application process:

  • There is no common deadline for submitting the 105 applications.
  • Deadlines differentiate from a university to another, and sometimes they depend on the applied programs.
    • Visit the university websites or contact the universities directly for their deadlines.
  • Note: if the deadline has passed and you are interested in a specific program, you can contact the university’s admission office for guidance.
International students and adult learners

You will need to fill out a 105F online application through the Ontario Universities’ Application Centre.

Timeline for the application process:

The timeline is similar to 105D applications

  • There is no common deadline for submitting the 105 applications.
  • Deadlines differentiate from a university to another, and sometimes they depend on the applied programs.
    • Visit the university websites or contact the universities directly for their deadlines.
  • Note: if the deadline has passed and you are interested in a specific program, you can contact the university’s admission office for guidance.

My Story ;)




My Experience

Applying to universities was stressful especially for an immigrant like me who's the oldest child in the family with no prior knowledge and guidance about the application process. Being provided with the OUAC student's number and related needed information to start my applications was something new that I was never told about before. I was stressed and anxious, but with doing my research about universities, applications, and different student experiences relieved the burden. I have received my offers very late compared to my peers and I was so anxious; I would like to comfort you if you are going through a similar experience, it's ok you will get your Offers sooner or later! I am now studying Software Engineering at the University of Guelph :)
Advice: if you are unsure about which program or university you would like to attend then try to join tours to learn more about them!
